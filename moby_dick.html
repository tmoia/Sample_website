Moby Dick: A classic tale of the sea

Moby Dick by Herman Melville begins with these immortal words:

Call me Ishmael. Some years ago-never mind how long precisely-having a little or no money in my purse, and nothing particularly to interest me on shore, I thought I would sail about a little and see the watery part of the world. It is a way I have of driving off the spleen and regulating the circulation.

After driving off hid spleen (which can't be good for you), Ishmael then goes on in much the same vein for approximately one jillion pages. The only thing bigger than Moby Dick (who spoiler alert!-is a giant white whale) is the bool itself.
